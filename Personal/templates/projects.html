My Computer Science Adventures

I have always been fascinated by the applications of Computer Science. During the summers of 2015 and 2016, I interned at AESTE, a chip design company. I was exposed to cryptography and electrical engineering from an industry perspective. I helped to develop a Certificate Management System for the company’s web app using OpenSSL, applying knowledge of both algorithms and web development. In the summer of 2018, I attained a research internship at a Deep Learning Laboratory at Universiti Tunku Abdul Rahman, where I developed a mobile app to house a wood identification model. I also learnt about different types of neural networks and their applications. I also built this website from scratch with Django and Bootstrap 4.

How Music Has Impacted Me

I have been playing the trumpet since 3rd grade. I have performed in my high school’s orchestra, jazz ensemble and brass quintet. Playing in musical ensembles has given me a chance to connect with my peers emotively and to be totally immersed in the musical rapport we create. I have also performed Handel's Messiah as a part of a professional baroque orchestra. As a project for my computer science class, I made a Markov Chain Music Generator, which took 15 Bach Inventions and attempted to create a new one in a desired key. These experiences have improved my discipline and time management skills, and I also have a greater sense of appreciation for music. I enjoy listening to Jazz, Classical, Metal and EDM.

Coxing

Rowing is the perfect sport for a STEM enthusiast to analyze rhythms. Back on land, training is done primarily on rowing machines which record data about each stroke pulled. As such, data is abundant. I brought my passion for computer science into the sport by helping to devise a system to automate the tedious task of data collection by coxswains, simultaneously opening up a plethora of new methods for coaches to find rowers who row best together.
